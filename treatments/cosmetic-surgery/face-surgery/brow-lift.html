What Is Brow Lift Surgery?
Eyebrows are one of the main structures that add meaning to our face and determine our facial expression. Although the structure and shape of our eyebrows are determined congenitally, changes in the sub-brow fat tissue, connective tissue and bone tissue over time can cause significant lowering of our eyebrows.
Eyebrow drooping not only impairs the aesthetic appearance, but also prevents the quality of vision by piling up on the eyelid. In this case, it is possible to get rid of the feeling of heaviness on the eye while correcting the tired, unhappy appearance with eyebrow lifting surgery.

Many patients who have undergone brow lift surgery due to excessive drooping of the eyebrows also state that their world has become brighter after the surgery and that their visual field has expanded greatly.
In addition to upper and lower lid surgeries, brow lift operations are also performed for visual function and opening the visual field in patients with facial paralysis or cutting injuries and who develop permanent damage.

What Are Eyebrow Lift Methods?
There are many surgical and non-surgical eyebrow lifting methods in accordance with the anatomical structure and needs of the person. Non-surgical methods include botulinum toxin, filling, thread applications and device procedures.
Among the surgical methods, there are different techniques that are made by entering from the eyelid fold, just above the eyebrow, or from the hairline.
Brow lift surgeries are mostly combined with eyelid surgeries; Eyelid surgeries in which the eyebrows are not raised, although necessary, may cause patients not to be happy enough.
Combining brow lift surgeries with eyelid aesthetic surgeries provides longer-term youth to both the eyelids, eyebrows and forehead.
A frequently used method in eyebrow lift surgery is the indirect method, which is performed through the same hidden incision during blepharoplasty surgery, where excess eyelid skin is removed. In this case, since a new incision is not made, there is no possibility of scarring. The outer part of the eyebrow is released upwards through the lid and attached to the tissue on the bone from the inside at the desired height. In this method, only the outer part of the eyebrow can be raised.
Raising the eyebrow with the incision just above the eyebrow provides the most effective and longest lasting result. However, this method, which has the possibility of scarring, requires appropriate patient selection and very serious experience. In this method, in which direct tissue is removed, the intended amount of elevation and the desired eyebrow shape can be obtained mathematically. In this method, only the outer part or the whole of the eyebrow can be raised. In most cases, an experienced surgeon can provide scar camouflage with special techniques and, if necessary, post-operative injections, and there is no scar problem in the long run.
In eyebrow and forehead lift, a very effective lift can be made with lengthwise incisions made just in front of or just behind the scalp. However, these procedures, in which the entire forehead is raised, are not preferred today for some reasons such as requiring a long recovery period and causing loss of sensation, and are only recommended for a small number of suitable patients.
Less invasive methods have been developed to protect the nerves with the help of an endoscopic camera by entering through small incisions made in the scalp in eyebrow lifting. Although they have a shorter duration of effectiveness compared to surgeries performed with incisions directly over the eyebrow, they are still effective for years; Endoscopic methods have advantages such as the ability to remove the entire forehead and the absence of scarring.

How Is Brow Lift Surgery Performed?
Brow lift operations are performed under local anesthesia or drowsiness anesthesia (sedation). General anesthesia is generally preferred in endoscopic methods. According to the surgical technique, incisions can be made with scalpel (steel blade), electrocautery, radiofrequency devices. Each technique has a different suture material and fixation method suitable for it.
In the indirect method performed through the eyelid, after descending from the fold incision to the appropriate tissue level, the outer half of the eyebrow is released from the bone by moving upwards in a bloodless plane. It is fixed to the semi-movable tissue on the bone with 1 or 2 permanent sutures by lifting the eyebrow up to the level determined during the examination. Then, the hidden incision in the lid fold is closed with sutures.
In eyebrow raising surgery to be performed directly over the eyebrow, a superficial incision is made just above the hairy part of the eyebrow and the incision is deepened with a modified method that will not damage the hair follicles. In this way, both the eyebrow hair loss is prevented and the scar camouflage is provided, and the surgeon's experience is very important in this sense. A predetermined amount of tissue is removed with the examination, and first the deep tissue is closed with embedded sutures, and then the skin tissue is closed with cosmetic stitches. Trace-preventing Z-plasty techniques are used at the incision tips. The surgery is terminated by sticking sterile bands on the incision sites.
In endoscopic brow lift surgeries, small incisions are made in the scalp. After descending to the tissue plane through these incisions, all forehead tissue and eyebrows are released by entering the endoscope and observing the nerves and vessels under direct imaging. Then, the eyebrow and forehead tissues are fixed by raising them with different methods using stitches or fixation implants. The incisions are closed with stitches or staples. A bandage is applied to the forehead area.
Regardless of the technique, eye closure is not possible after the surgery, the patient can be discharged after 1-2 hours of observation and ice application after the surgery.

How Is The Recovery After Brow Lift Surgery?
The recovery period after brow lift surgery is usually uneventful. The patient can continue with his daily activities that do not require excessive effort. As in any surgery, there is a possibility of swelling and bruising, it varies between 1-2 days and 1-2 weeks, depending on the method. Significant pain is not expected during this period, and difficulties may be experienced in moving the eyebrows for the first 1-2 days. In the endoscopic method, there may be a temporary loss of sensation on the forehead. It is generally recommended not to touch the wounds with water for 5 days, sutures 7-10. taken in days.

Is Non-Surgical Brow Lift Possible?
It is also possible to raise the sagging eyebrow tissue with non-surgical methods in suitable patients. However, these methods are not suitable for everyone and if, after a detailed evaluation, it is decided that surgery is required, it is necessary not to waste time with these methods.

What Are Non-Surgical Brow Lift Methods?
Non-surgical eyebrow lifting and raising methods;
Botulinum toxin applications,
Filling applications,
rope hangers,
Device methods are included.
Botulinum toxin is an application in the hands of an experienced practitioner that allows shaping the eyebrows and eye area as desired. By relaxing the opposing muscles, it is possible to raise or lower the outer part of the eyebrow or all of it. Its effect is limited in very heavy eyebrow tissues or obvious skin sagging.
When the connective tissue of the eyebrow is loosened or the fat tissue underneath is reduced, hyaluronic acid filling applications that support the tissues can be beneficial. In this application, the selection and application technique of a filling material suitable for the patient's structure and the desired result is critical.
Although it is possible to raise the eyebrows with rope hangers, the effect time is quite short. The tension obtained in the first months can quickly succumb to gravity. For this reason, it can be applied as an option in patients who do not have heavy eyebrow tissue and do not want surgery, but who want to increase their eyebrow tissue even for a short time.
Device applications aiming to tighten the collagen of the forehead tissue on the eyebrow and the lifting frontalis muscle can also provide temporary eyebrow lift. These include devices that use ultrasonic waves (sound waves) applied from the surface and radiofrequency energy that can be applied from deep tissue. Devices using bipolar radiofrequency energy, which have been developed recently, can remove both superficial and deep forehead tissues effectively and for a long time, by entering through a small hole just under the scalp. It has advantages such as not requiring a recovery period, no possibility of scarring, and being able to return to normal life immediately after a very short application.

Brow Lift Price
Eyebrow lift procedures and surgeries are performed with many different methods depending on the anatomy of the person and the amount of need, and which method is suitable and whether additional procedures are needed is determined by a detailed examination. Therefore, the procedure to be applied to everyone is different and the price information for eyebrow lifting can only be determined after a free consultation.

At What Age Is Brow Lift Surgery Performed?
In fact, our eyebrow structure is determined by genetic factors and the eyebrows of the person can be low even at very young ages. However, especially low eyebrows at a level that prevents vision occur due to tissue laxity that develops as age progresses, and surgery is demanded in advancing ages.
Many patients in their twenties and thirties who look extremely unhappy and tired due to the excessively low outer part of the eyebrow, and this situation is reflected in their social relations or performance at work, also benefit greatly from eyebrow lifting surgery and are much happier and more productive in their social and business lives after the procedure.

Is Brow Lift Surgery Permanent?
The duration of effect of brow lift surgeries varies between 1-2 years and 20 years, depending on the structure of the patient's tissues, the aging process and the chosen surgical technique. In this regard, the longest effect can be achieved with the direct method just above the eyebrow.

Which Doctor Performs Brow Lift Surgery?
Brow lift operations can also be performed by oculoplastic surgeons who have been trained in aesthetic and functional surgeries of all tissues around the eyes, as well as by doctors from other branches who have experience in eyebrow surgeries.
