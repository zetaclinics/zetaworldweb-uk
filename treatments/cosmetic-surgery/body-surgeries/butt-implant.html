Butt Implant

What is Butt Implant?
“Total Butt Arthroplasty”, also known as total butt implant, is an operation to replace the damaged joint, which is applied to patients whose hip joint is severely calcified (osteoarthritis) or damaged.

For Which Diseases Is Total Butt Implant Used?
Total butt implant surgery is usually performed after middle age. There is no upper age limit for this operation. It can be applied to anyone who has completed or is about to complete bone development. Butt implant surgery; It is a surgery known to offer definitive solutions for people in the 20-40 age group, especially in cases such as hip dislocation called developmental "hip dysplasia" or bleeding of the femoral head called "avascular necrosis".
We can list the diseases and disorders that are applied to hip prosthesis in general as follows:
Calcification,
Tumors,
Disease sequelae from childhood,
Rheumatic diseases,
Inflammation sequelae,
Bone necrosis that occurs after hip fractures and blood supply problems with advancing age.
People suffering from these diseases and conditions are good candidates for butt implant surgery. However, non-surgical treatments are primarily applied. If non-surgical treatments have not been beneficial or are not expected to be beneficial, then butt implant surgery is considered.

How is Butt Implant Surgery Performed?
If the patient does not have an active infection such as urinary tract or throat infection, blood samples are taken to start the preparations for the surgery. Then the anesthesiologist is consulted. If there is no obstacle to the realization of the operation, the patient is admitted to the hospital the day before the operation. Diabetes and blood pressure diseases do not constitute an obstacle for butt implant surgery. However, these patients should be kept under close follow-up. In addition, smoking is not recommended as it will increase the risk of infection.
The operation can be performed under anesthesia, known as general anesthesia, or by anesthetizing the waist. Depending on the surgeon's preference, a 10-20 cm skin incision made from the hip is also used. At this stage of the surgery, the damaged bones are removed from the hip and replaced with prostheses. Then suturing is done for the capsule, fascia, muscle, skin and subcutaneous tissues. After an average of 4-6 hours after the operation, the patient is allowed to eat normally by mouth. On the evening of the operation or the day after the operation, patients begin to walk with support. In this process, it is essential for patients to use compression stockings and walkers.
It is useful to pay attention to the following details after the operation:
Take care not to cross your legs for at least 2 months.
Do not lean forward and try to pick up anything from the ground while sitting.
Try not to raise your knees above hip level.
If possible, do not sit on the Turkish style toilet.
Try not to lean too far forward when sitting and standing.

What are the Complications that May Occur After Butt Implant Surgery?
The complication rate after total butt implant surgery is very low. The most common complication is the clot that forms in the veins with the slowing of the blood flow in the leg. In order to prevent this, preventive treatment with drugs that will dilute the blood is put into action after the surgical intervention. This preventive treatment can be extended up to 20 days if necessary. Starting walks and avoiding inactivity in the early postoperative period will greatly reduce the likelihood of this risk occurring. In addition, it may be beneficial for the patient to use compression stockings at this stage.
Infection is the most feared in butt implant operations; Therefore, it is the issue that the most effort is made to prevent it from happening. In case of infection, even the prosthesis is changed. In addition, long-term use of antibiotics becomes necessary. Performing the surgery in a good center by surgeons who are experts in the field greatly reduces the risk of infection. The life of the prosthesis is also expected to be long, but if it is worked under heavy conditions for a long time, the life of the prosthesis is gradually shortened. At the same time, premature wear may occur in the prosthetic spacers due to this situation. Small particles due to wear may accumulate at the bone and prosthesis interface and cause loosening of the prosthesis. In such a case, the prosthesis should be changed. If the prosthesis is not replaced, it may cause bone resorption. Apart from these; Complications such as dislocation of the prosthesis, fractures around the prosthesis, fractures in the prosthesis itself, and vascular and nerve injury may also occur. However, the likelihood of these complications occurring is extremely low.

Frequently Asked Questions About Butt Implant Surgery
What Symptoms Are Seen in Butt Implant Candidates?
The most common symptom in butt implant candidates is severe pain. The pain, which occurs only when you walk in the first place, starts to bother you even when you rest in the later process. Apart from this, we can list other symptoms as follows:
limitation of movement,
Short leg,
Limping.

What Happens If We Delay Butt Implant Surgery?
When it comes to butt implant surgery, non-surgical treatments may also be possible. Physical therapy applications, medications, using a cane, injections for the joint such as PRP or stem cells are among the non-surgical options. However, if surgical treatment is required but treatment is delayed, serious calcification and deterioration risk may occur even in the two intact knees, other hips, and even back and waist regions. If the operation is not performed, the risk for other parts of the body also increases, as other parts of the body will be more burdened than normal.

Who Can Not Be Applied Butt Prosthesis?
Butt implant surgery;
Individuals with poor personal hygiene
People with dementia or mental illness
People with active infection in the hip area,
People with advanced insufficiency in arteries or veins,
Hip circumference is not suitable for individuals with muscle paralysis related to neurological diseases.

What Is The Service Time of the Butt Implant?
If all the right conditions are met, the butt implant is intended to last a lifetime. Although there are many factors that determine the service life of the prosthesis, it is expected that the prosthesis will last for at least 15 years if the quality is not ignored. It is also possible for this period to be 30 years or more.

Can We Run After Butt Implant Surgery?
Light sports activities such as running can be started four months after the surgery.

When Is The Earliest We Can Take a Bath After Butt Implant Surgery?
Patients can start taking a bath two weeks after the surgery.
